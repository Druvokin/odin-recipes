Kare-kare

Description

Kare-kare is a Philippine stew (kare derives from "curry") that features a thick savory peanut sauce. It is generally made from a base of stewed oxtail, beef tripe, pork hocks, calves' feet, pig's feet or trotters, various cuts of pork, beef stew meat, and occasionally offal.

Ingredients

  • oxtail
  • small banana flower bud
  • pechay or bok choy
  • string beans
  • eggplant
  • ground peanuts
  • peanut butter
  • shrimp paste
  • water
  • anatto seeds
  • toasted ground rice
  • garlic
  • onion
  • salt and pepper

Steps

  1. In a large pot, bring the water to a boil.
  2. Put in the oxtail followed by the onions and simmer for 2.5 to 3 hrs or until tender (35 minutes if using a pressure cooker)
  3. Once the meat is tender, add the ground peanuts, peanut butter, and coloring (water from annatto seed mixture) and simmer for 5 to 7 minutes
  4. Add the toasted ground rice and simmer for 5 minutes
  5. On a seperate pan, saute the garlic then add the banana flower, eggplant and string beans and cook for 5 minutes
  6. Transfer the cooked vegetables to the large pot (where the rest of the ingredients are)
  7. Add salt and pepper to taste
  8. Serve hot with shrimp paste. Enjoy!
